# Fournil - Formula Reference

Extracted from Andrew Janjigian's Sourdough Lifestyle Workshop Handout.

## Baker's Math

**Baker's percentages** = each ingredient as a ratio by weight relative to total flour (flour = 100%).

### Converting TO Baker's Percentages
- Divide each ingredient weight by total flour weight
- Convert to percentage (move decimal two places right)
- Multiple flours: combine total weight first, then calculate individual %

### Converting FROM Baker's Percentages (Resizing)
1. Sum all percentages to get `totalPercentage`
2. `conversionFactor = desiredDoughWeight / totalPercentage`
3. Each ingredient grams = `percentage × conversionFactor`
4. Round UP (a little extra is better than too little)

### Hydration
- Hydration = water % relative to flour
- 75% hydration = 75g water per 100g flour
- Different flours absorb water differently (whole wheat absorbs more)

### Preferments (Levain)
- 100% hydration levain = 50% flour + 50% water by weight
- Must break out flour and water from levain for correct overall formula
- Example: 50g levain at 100% hydration = 25g flour + 25g water

## DDT (Desired Dough Temperature) Formula

Target DDT for sourdough: **78°F**

```
A = 78 - [flour temperature]
B = 78 - [room/bowl temperature]
C = 78 - [friction factor]
D = (78 - [preferment temperature]) × [preferment %]
Final water temperature = A + B + C + D
```

**Friction factors:**
- Hand mixing: 5
- Stand mixer: 25
- Food processor: 30

### Simplified DDT for Levain Builds
```
A = 78 - [flour temperature]
B = (78 - [preferment temperature]) × 50%
C = 78 (the DDT itself)
Water temperature = A + B + C
```

## Starter Care

### Basic Refreshment (2:2:1)
- 150g flour, 150g water, 75g starter
- Proof at 78°F until doubled (4-6 hours)
- Refrigerate for up to 14 days (best within 7-10)

### Slow Overnight Build (20:20:1)
- 150g flour, 150g water, 8g starter
- Leave at 75-78°F until doubled (12-14 hours)
- Use cold water (60°F) when ambient temps are high

### Reviving a Flagging Starter
**Phase 1** (repeat until doubling within 6-8 hours, ~2-3 days):
- Morning (7am, 1:1:1): 25g bread flour/1.5g rye/25g 80°F water/25g starter
- Afternoon (2pm, 1:1:1): same
- Overnight (9pm, 5:5:1): 25g bread flour/1.5g rye/25g 65°F water/5g starter

**Phase 2** (repeat until clearly doubling in 4-6 hours):
- Morning (7am, 2:2:1): 25g bread flour/1.5g rye/25g 80°F water/12g starter
- Afternoon (2pm, 2:2:1): same
- Overnight (9pm, 5:5:1): 25g bread flour/1.5g rye/25g 65°F water/5g starter

## Built-in Formulas

### No-Knead Pain au Levain
**Overall Formula:**
- 95% high-protein AP or bread flour
- 5% rye flour (or whole wheat)
- 74% cool (75°F) water
- 2.2% salt
- 1-10% levain (5% is standard)

**Final Formula (5% levain):**
- 360g water, 475g AP flour, 25g rye flour, 25g levain, 11g salt
- Note: 1% levain = 7g; 10% levain = 66g

**Timeline (~22 hours):**
0:00 Mix (autolyse) → 0:30 Add salt → 1:00 Coil fold → 13:00 Preshape → 13:30 Final shape → 14:00 Fridge (8-24h) → 22:00 Bake

### Four-Fold Pain au Levain
**Overall Formula:**
- 95% AP or bread flour
- 5% rye flour
- 75-80% water (less for beginners, more once you are ready)
- 2.2% salt

**Difficulty by variant:**
- 75% hydration = Beginner (forgiving dough, easy to shape)
- 80% hydration = Intermediate-Advanced (stickier, harder to shape, more open crumb)
- Higher levain % = faster fermentation (adjust for kitchen temperature)
- 12.5-35% levain (25% for normal temps)

**Pre-calculated Final Formulas:**

| Hydration | Levain % | Water | AP Flour | Rye | Levain | Salt |
|-----------|----------|-------|----------|-----|--------|------|
| 75% | 25% | 315g | 420g | 25g | 130g | 11g |
| 75% | 35% | 290g | 395g | 25g | 180g | 11g |
| 80% | 25% | 330g | 405g | 25g | 125g | 11g |
| 80% | 35% | 310g | 380g | 25g | 175g | 11g |

**Levain Build Options:**
- Quick (50% starter): 100g flour, 100g water, 50g starter (4-6h)
- Overnight (5% starter): 100g flour, 100g water, 5g starter (12-14h)

**Timeline (~16.5 hours):**
0:00 Feed levain (4-6h) → 3:00 Autolyse (1h) → 4:00 Add levain+salt → 4:45 Coil fold → 5:30 Coil fold → 6:15 Coil fold → 7:00 Coil fold (until 60-100% expanded) → 8:30 Preshape → 9:00 Final shape → 9:30 Fridge (8-24h) → 16:30 Bake

### Tartine Basic Country Bread
- 90% bread flour, 10% whole wheat
- 75% water
- 2% salt
- 20% leaven (young, sweet-smelling, low acidity)
- Based on 1kg total flour

## Dutch Oven Baking
1. Preheat Dutch oven at **475°F for 1 hour**, highest rack
2. Lay parchment strip on loaf
3. Invert loaf onto counter
4. Remove pot, set lid aside
5. Score swiftly and confidently
6. Transfer to pot via parchment
7. Add 1-2 small ice cubes (optional, avoid touching loaf)
8. Cover, return to oven
9. **Bake covered 20 minutes**
10. Remove pot, remove loaf to bare rack/stone
11. Reduce to **425°F**
12. **Bake uncovered 15-30 minutes** until deep brown

## Glossary
- **Autolyse:** 15-60 min rest before salt addition; hydrates flour, develops gluten passively
- **Bulk proof:** Fermentation before dividing and shaping
- **DDT:** Desired dough temperature (78°F for sourdough)
- **Final formula:** Ingredients mixed into dough, minus preferment ingredients
- **Final proof:** Fermentation after dividing and shaping
- **Friction factor:** Heat from mixing method (5 hand, 25 mixer, 30 processor)
- **Hydration:** Water-to-flour ratio (75% = 75g water per 100g flour)
- **Levain:** French for sourdough culture; often used for the build made for a specific dough
- **Levain build:** The preferment stage of sourdough fermentation
- **Overall formula:** Combined ratio of all ingredients
- **Preferment:** Dough portion fermented before final mix (levain, biga, poolish)
- **Prefermented flour:** % of flour in the dough that starts in the preferment
- **Preshape:** Initial "partial" shaping followed by rest before final shape
- **Cold-proofing:** Slowing fermentation via refrigeration (better flavor, easier shaping, better scoring)
- **Starter:** Sourdough culture used to "start" a levain
